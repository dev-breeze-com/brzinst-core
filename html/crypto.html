Breeze::OS Installer -- Drive Encryption Schemes

To the left are settings for hard-drive encryption schemes.
The schemes offered are dm-crypt or encfs.

  • dm-crypt a transparent disk encryption subsystem.
  • encfs an encrypted filesystem in user-space.
The scheme dm-crypt provides block device encryption; and,
can be used to encrypt your entire drive. encfs is a stacked
cryptographic filesystem; and, is used to encrypt folders and files.

The encfs scheme is the easiest to use; but, is slower than dm-crypt.
It is used to encrypt your home folder, and optionally, your share folder.
The encryption scheme encfs is not offered in this version.

You must enter a password for decryption.

The recommended encryption scheme is dm-crypt [luks].
You may choose to use one keyfile per partition, or one for all partitions.
You are also given the choice to erase each partition to be encrypted.

Beware, partition erasure is an extremely slow process.
30 minutes on average for a 20G partition with 3 iterations.
