Breeze::OS Installer -- Logical Volume Manager

The Logical Volume Manager (LVM) is used to map the partitions
on your hard-drives, as physical and logical volumes, which are
independent of the underlying layout of your hard-drives.
It facilitates the resizing of formatted partitions.

  • A physical volume can be created using any block device.
  • A volume group is allocated from physical volumes.
  • A logical volume is allocated from a volume group.
Installer steps required to enable LVM partitions:
  • Create physical volumes from physical partitions,
  • Create volume groups with the physical volumes,
  • Create logical volumes from physical volumes and volume groups.
  • Format logical volumes to setup an LVM context for your drives.

The Breeze::OS©® LVM scheme:
Our schemes create only two volume groups, vgswap and vgstore.
All logical volumes except for swap are allocated from vgstore.
Our LVM partitioning scheme requires a separate boot partition;
therefore, will create your LVM setup from scratch.
If you choose to include several hard-drives within an LVM context; then,
you must partition every hard-drive with the same partitioning scheme.

You may go back to drives to partition another hard-drive; or,
continue by first selecting one or more LVM partitioned drives on the left.
