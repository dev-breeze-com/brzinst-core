>Breeze::OS Installer -- Virtual Clubs & Internet

You must have a dynamic domain name service (DNS) account, so that you can communicate
with other people on the Internet. A dynamic DNS account is not required, if you have
a static Internet IP address from your Internet service provider.

Once you have a dynamic DNS account, you can then use your dynamic DNS address to communicate with friends and colleagues. When creating your account; you must provide the fully qualified domain name, consisting of host and domain names; as well as, username and password.

You can communicate by simply using the dynamic DNS address to issue requests; or,
by creating virtual clubs with limited access to peers. Your dynamic DNS address is used as
your publicly visible fully qualified domain name (FQDN) on the Internet.
